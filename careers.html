Careers in Clean Skies & Desert Signals

From satellites to sensors — learn, apply, and protect our planet

Each module in the autonomous_space system represents a real-world discipline — from satellite engineering to public policy. Here’s how you can start learning, and where these careers exist today.

Satellite & Space Systems Engineer

What they do: Design, test, and maintain satellites, sensors, and data systems for space and Earth observation missions.

  • Skills: Aerospace systems, orbital mechanics, control systems, RF communications.
  • Education: Aerospace Engineering, Systems Engineering, Electrical Engineering.
  • Top Schools: MIT, Georgia Tech, Purdue, University of Arizona.
  • Careers: NASA Careers, USAJOBS Satellite Engineer.
Drone / Robotics Systems Engineer

What they do: Develop drones, sensors, and autonomous robotics systems for monitoring, mapping, and safety.

  • Skills: Control systems, computer vision (YOLO), path planning, embedded systems.
  • Education: Robotics, Computer Engineering, Mechatronics.
  • Top Schools: Carnegie Mellon University, University of Pennsylvania, MIT Robotics.
  • Careers: NASA Robotics Jobs, Lockheed Martin Careers.
Earth & Climate Data Scientist

What they do: Analyze Earth observation data (satellite + ground), model drought and climate risks, and support policy or urban planning.

  • Skills: Python, GIS, machine learning, hydrology, remote sensing.
  • Education: Earth Science, Environmental Engineering, Data Science.
  • Top Schools: University of Colorado Boulder, Stanford Earth, UMass Amherst.
  • Careers: NASA Earthdata, NOAA Careers, USGS Careers.
Communications & Crypto Engineer

What they do: Secure and optimize communication between satellites, drones, and agencies using encryption and post-quantum protocols.

  • Skills: RF systems, network security, quantum-safe encryption, signal processing.
  • Education: Electrical Engineering, Cybersecurity, Computer Engineering.
  • Top Schools: Georgia Tech ECE, University of Missouri Cyber Institute, University of Maryland.
  • Careers: NIST PQC Research, NASA Network Engineering Jobs.
Policy, Safety & Mission Assurance Specialist

What they do: Manage mission safety, ethical AI integration, environmental compliance, and inter-agency coordination.

  • Skills: Systems management, policy analysis, ethics, aerospace operations.
  • Education: Public Policy, Aerospace Systems, or Risk Engineering.
  • Careers: NASA Safety & Mission Assurance (OSMA), USAJOBS Safety Engineer.

Quick Roadmap to a Space & Climate Tech Career

  • 1. Learn the Basics (0–1 year): Take free NASA or Coursera data science and aerospace intro courses. Practice Python, GIS, or robotics kits.
  • 2. Build Skills (1–3 years): Work on small projects — NASA open APIs, weather data visualizations, or drone flight simulations.
  • 3. Get Certified (2–4 years): Earn degrees or certificates in aerospace, robotics, environmental data, or cybersecurity.
  • 4. Apply or Intern (3–5 years): Apply via NASA Internships, NOAA, or USGS field internships.
  • 5. Advance (5+ years): Join full-time roles at NASA, JPL, NOAA, or startups in climate and space tech.
  • 6. Optional: Coursera courses for upskilling. Sign up here: Upskill with Coursera and explore and learn many skills.
